The math under the feathers

Every lane has a survival chance, and the multiplier for reaching it is roughly the inverse of your combined chance of getting there, adjusted by the house margin. That adjustment is the entire business model: whether you cash out after one lane or ten, the long-run value of the round stays a few percent below your stake.

The practical result is simple: there is no mathematically better stopping point. Banking early trades top-end value for frequency; banking late does the opposite — a matter of variance and temperament, never a way to beat the margin in the Chicken road game casino format.
